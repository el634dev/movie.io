Bruce Lee

Philanthropist & Martial Artist

Here's a timeline of Lee's life:

  • 1940 - Born in San Franciso, California
  • 1950 - Co star in his first movie The Kid alongside his father
  • 1956 - Transfered to St. Francis Xavier's College and mentored by Brother Edward, the school's boxing coach. Was introduced to Wip Man and rejected at first because of one quarter German background but later trained privately.
  • 1958 - Won Hong Kong school's boxing tournament and Hong Kong's Crown Colony Cha-Cha championship
  • 1959 - Opened his first martial arts school in Seattle
  • 1961 - Enrolled in the University of Washington and studied various subjects
  • 1964 - Dropped out of college and opened his second martial arts school in Oakland
  • 1966/1967 - Starred in the tv show The Green Hornet and also appeared in three crossover epsiodes of Batman. Opened The Jun Fan Gung Fu Institute.
  • 1971 - Appeared in Longstreet and played his first leading role
  • 1972 - Worked on his fourth film and starred in Enter the Dragon
  • 1973 - Dies at the age of 32

Lee was named one of 100 most important people of the 20th century and continues to be an influence on modern combat sports. Lee was also credited with changing the way Asians are viewed in American films.
